Should schools require uniforms?

Isaiah: Pro Uniforms

I think schools should require uniforms. The reasons for that are because it creates unity, prepares students for a professional workplace and removes inappropiate wardrobe issues.

First of all, students wearing uniforms creates unity beacuse everyone will look the same and be equal.

Second of all, students wearing uniforms will prapare students for professional workplace. Students will be used to dressing formal and will be more likely to be hired.

Lastly, students wearing uniforms will remove inappropriate wardrobe issues. There will be no need to enforce a dress code and worry about what students wear.

Josh: Anti Uniforms

I believe college uniforms should not be mandatory for a number of reasons.

Not only can we not expect every student to afford being able to dress professionally every week but I also doubt any positive effects would be significant.

Putting on a uniform won’t change people enough to reduce bullying and a loosely worn uniform won’t exude professionalism. While people tend to act more professional in a setting where they dress professionally I find the setting has more to do with that behaviour than their wardrobe. A room full of students will still talk with their friends wether or not they’re in uniform.

While I understand the benefits of dressing professionally I find it unnecessary for even day classes. I wouldn’t be against it for special occasions, graduation or large student gatherings, but daily uniforms is excessive.
